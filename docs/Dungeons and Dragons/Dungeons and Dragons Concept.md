## **The Game Concept: The 300-Day Deluge**

### **Scenario Overview**
The world faces an **apocalyptic catastrophe** where **water levels are rising inexorably**. In **300 days**, the entire world will be submerged beneath the waves. This is not merely a physical crisis—it represents the **collapse of civilization's foundational structures**: energy grids, communication networks, technological infrastructure, knowledge repositories, and temporal continuity.

### **Primary Objective**
Players must **survive** by:
1. **Finding or creating a sanctuary** (high ground, floating city, underwater habitat)
2. **Building a "very powerful boat"** capable of sustaining life indefinitely
3. **Preserving the Five Pillars** of civilization (Energy, Communication, Knowledge, Time, Technology)

### **Alternative Scenarios**
- **Zombie Apocalypse Variant:** Undead hordes replace rising waters; players must scavenge, escape, and fortify
- **Political Collapse Variant:** Societal breakdown without supernatural elements; focus on resource management and diplomacy
- **Cosmic Event Variant:** Reality itself is fragmenting; players must navigate spacetime anomalies

### **2. The Five Dining Philosophers as Character Stats**

Replacing traditional D&D ability scores (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma), characters are defined by **The Five Gods**:

#### **Energy (ENE)** - *God of Energy*
- **Function:** Physical vitality, stamina, power generation
- **Influences:** Combat endurance, resource extraction, power system maintenance
- **Traditional D&D Equivalent:** Constitution + Strength
- **Example:** A character with ENE 18 can operate a manual generator for hours without fatigue

#### **Communication (COM)** - *God of Communication*
- **Function:** Information exchange, persuasion, coordination
- **Influences:** Diplomacy, team synergy, signal transmission, cultural understanding
- **Traditional D&D Equivalent:** Charisma + Wisdom (social aspects)
- **Example:** COM 16 allows successful negotiation with rival survivor groups

#### **Knowledge (KNO)** - *God of Knowledge*
- **Function:** Learning, memory, analysis, wisdom
- **Influences:** Problem-solving, research, identifying patterns, historical insight
- **Traditional D&D Equivalent:** Intelligence + Wisdom (analytical aspects)
- **Example:** KNO 15 enables deciphering ancient blueprints for water purification

#### **Time (TIM)** - *God of Time*
- **Function:** Temporal awareness, planning, reaction speed
- **Influences:** Initiative, scheduling efficiency, predicting outcomes, aging resistance
- **Traditional D&D Equivalent:** Dexterity (reflexes) + Wisdom (foresight)
- **Example:** TIM 17 grants awareness of the exact days remaining and optimal task sequencing

#### **Technology (TEC)** - *God of Technology*
- **Function:** Tool creation, system operation, innovation
- **Influences:** Crafting, machinery repair, invention, digital systems
- **Traditional D&D Equivalent:** Intelligence (applied) + Dexterity (precision)
- **Example:** TEC 19 allows construction of a quantum computer from salvaged parts

## **Gameplay Mechanics: The d20 Protocol**

### **Core Resolution System**
Like traditional D&D, the game uses **d20 rolls** to determine uncertain outcomes:

1. **Roll 1d20** (twenty-sided die)
2. **Add relevant stat modifier** (ENE, COM, KNO, TIM, or TEC)
3. **Add proficiency bonus** (if character has training in that skill)
4. **Compare to Difficulty Class (DC)** set by the AI DM
5. **Success if Total ≥ DC**

### **Stat Modifiers**
Stat scores (1-20) convert to modifiers using the standard D&D formula:
```
Modifier = (Stat Score - 10) ÷ 2 (rounded down)

Examples:
ENE 18 → +4 modifier
COM 12 → +1 modifier
KNO 8 → -1 modifier
```

### **Unique Innovation: Auto-Success Threshold**
**If a character's base stat already exceeds the DC, they automatically succeed without rolling.**

This creates a **progression curve** where:
- Early game: Players roll for most actions
- Mid game: Players auto-succeed at basic tasks, roll for moderate challenges
- Late game: Players auto-succeed at moderate tasks, roll only for extreme challenges

**Example:**
- Repairing a basic radio requires TEC DC 12
- Character with TEC 14 (+2 modifier, base score 14) automatically succeeds
- Character with TEC 10 (+0 modifier) must roll d20 and get 12+ to succeed

### **Equipment and Progression**
Acquiring or building equipment **permanently increases stat values**:

| Equipment | Stat Boost | Example |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Solar Panel Array | +2 TEC | Enables advanced electronics |
| Communication Tower | +2 COM | Extends signal range |
| Library Archive | +2 KNO | Access to preserved knowledge |
| Chronometer Network | +2 TIM | Precise time tracking |
| Hydroelectric Generator | +2 ENE | Sustainable power source |

**Quantum Computer Example:**
- Requires TEC 18 to operate
- Building it requires TEC 15, KNO 14, and 50 days of work
- Once operational, grants +3 TEC and +2 KNO to the entire party
